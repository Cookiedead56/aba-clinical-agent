## I. Present Levels of Performance

**Date of Birth**: 2021-11-15 (4 years 2 months)
**Assessment Basis**: VB-MAPP 2026-01-12

### 1. Strength Areas

1. **Echoic (8/10)**: Single-word echoic accuracy approximately 70%, two-word phrase approximately 40%, CV/CVC/CVCV syllable structures basically mastered. This is the core lever for language expansion; echoic-to-mand/tact transfer strategies can rapidly build vocabulary.
2. **Visual Perceptual/MTS (8/10)**: Identical matching stable, non-identical matching (object-to-picture) approximately 80% accuracy. Visual learning channel is strong; instruction should leverage visual supports.
3. **Imitation (7/10)**: Gross motor imitation good (clapping, raising hands), fine motor approximately 60%, oral motor imitation adequate, providing foundation for echoic training.
4. **Mand (7/10)**: Primarily single-word mands, frequency can reach 5 per hour when motivation is clear.

### 2. Areas Requiring Intensive Intervention

#### 1) Social Interaction Quality
- Social Skills (3/10): No spontaneous peer interaction, only brief parallel play with high adult support, systematic avoidance when peers approach
- FBA confirms social avoidance function is escape (social negative reinforcement), baseline frequency 4-5 times/30 minutes
- Play (4/10): Primarily functional play, no pretend play, limited variety

#### 2) Expressive Language & Conversation
- Intraverbal (3/10): Cannot answer simple social questions ("What is your name?"), cannot engage in fill-in-the-blank conversation
- Tact (5/10): Can name approximately 15-20 common objects, but very few spontaneous tacts, action/attribute naming both absent
- LRFFC (4/10): By function approximately 30% correct, by feature approximately 20%, by category not yet established

#### 3) Group Skills & Classroom Adaptation
- Group Skills (3/10): First-response rate to group instructions approximately 30%, cannot wait for turns in group settings, sustained group participation approximately 2-3 minutes

### 3. Underlying Skill Diagnostic

| Underlying Skill | Current Status | Data Basis | Affected Higher Skills |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| Joint Attention | Partially ready | Eye contact brief (1-2 sec), only during high motivation | Group instruction following, social referencing, shared attention |
| Instructional Compliance | Partially ready | Good compliance with preferred tasks, escape with non-preferred | All tabletop teaching goal progression efficiency |
| Social Motivation | Significantly deficient | Near-zero peer interaction, systematic social avoidance | Social skill acquisition, inclusion transition |
| Prompt Dependency | Significantly deficient | Barriers assessment 3/4, low independent response rate | Skill generalization, independence |
| Auditory Attention | Partially ready | Attention/sensory barriers 2/4 | Group instruction response, intraverbal conversation |

### 4. Root Cause Analysis

> [!WARNING] Key Bottleneck 1: Social Avoidance Vicious Cycle
> - **Surface behavior**: Alex runs/flees when peers approach or group interaction is required (4-5 times/30 min)
> - **Root cause**: Severe social skill deficit (Social 3/10) -> Continuous "failure experiences" in social contexts -> Social interaction becomes conditioned aversive stimulus -> Avoidance maintained by negative reinforcement -> Loss of social learning opportunities -> Skill gap widens further
> - **Solution**: FCT teaching "I need a break" + Gradual social demand reduction + Embedding high-preference activities in social contexts -> Break the "avoid -> lose practice -> less competent -> more avoidance" cycle

> [!WARNING] Key Bottleneck 2: Prompt Dependency Blocking Independence
> - **Surface behavior**: Most skills require verbal/gestural prompts to produce, extremely low spontaneous response rate
> - **Root cause**: Previous intervention may have overused prompts without systematic fading, forming a "wait for prompt" response pattern
> - **Solution**: Systematic prompt hierarchy management (full prompt -> partial -> delayed -> independent), each goal has explicit prompt fading timeline
